Question from the podcast
"How did you scale cold traffic to $100K/day in under a month at V Shred?"
The first thing you need is a creative that destroys CPC. Kevin's philosophy was: the first 3 seconds of the ad is all that matters. Lower CPC to 50–70 cents and you can scale anything. At $2 CPC you're stuck.

Then use the lifetime budget method. Set your campaign to run 90 days. When you want to explode spend on a big day, shrink the end date to 30 days. Facebook recalculates to hit that budget faster — performance holds far better than raising the daily budget.
Segredos da Escala #147 · VTurb · ~1:17:00
Question from the podcast
"Facebook vs Google — how do you think about the two platforms when scaling a B2C offer?"
Facebook is a speedboat — turn left, turn right, instantly. Exit the learning phase and you can 10x the budget and it holds. Google is a cruise ship. Every turn needs planning weeks in advance. At V Shred, Facebook was 60% of spend and Google 40%. The key to winning on Google: figure out how to run your most aggressive creative there. Once you crack their approval patterns, your YouTube buyer has the highest LTV of any channel.
Segredos da Escala #147 · ~1:16:00
Question from the podcast
"V Shred hit $300M a year. What was the single most important factor?"
Everyone expects a tactical answer. But it was relationships. The relationship we built with Facebook directly gave us advantages most advertisers never see. Most marketers treat platforms as vending machines. The best operators treat them as long-term strategic partners.

Same with our agency Kendago — we paid serious commissions because they were serious partners. Then we built internal talent to take over each channel. Use agencies to learn, then build the capability in-house.
Segredos da Escala #147 · ~1:00:00 and ~2:00:00
Question from the podcast
"When do you give up on a struggling media buyer vs. keep investing in them?"
Look for signs of life. Good weeks mixed with bad weeks is different from someone flatlined. Use your external benchmark too: if the agency is winning and your internal person isn't — that's your problem.

I had a buyer who struggled for 6 months on TikTok. She eventually surpassed our agency. A Google buyer took 7–8 months. Patience with the right person always paid off.

"How did you get promoted to Director at V Shred?"
I started in retargeting. Within a month I'd scaled cold traffic to $100K/day — more than all 6 other buyers combined. I walked into Kevin's office: "Let me manage this team." He asked what title I wanted. I said Director.

The lesson: don't wait to be offered authority. Identify the gap, fill it, make the ask. Authority is earned by doing, then claimed by asking.

Question from the podcast
"How did you structure the creative team to out-test everyone else?"
The ideal structure: one media buyer paired with one dedicated video editor. The buyer focused on what was working; the editor executed immediately. The copywriter gave direction at the start, then got out of the way.

Kevin's mantra: "We're always one ad away." An egg cracked into a pan. Avocado on a cutting board. Kevin hired someone in the Philippines to film strange food combinations at the market. Millions of dollars from that footage.

Question from the podcast
"What was the hardest part of moving from individual contributor to managing a team of 20?"
Accepting that your job is no longer to be the best media buyer in the room — it's to find and develop people who are better than you were. That's a real identity shift.

I tried to reposition people rather than fire them. A project manager missing deadlines but obsessed with reports? Moved him to data. A buyer struggling on Facebook after 5 years? Moved her to TikTok. Management is reading what people actually want, not just what they say.
Segredos da Escala #147 · ~2:06:00
Question from the podcast
"Quizzes are dominating US performance marketing. Why?"
It's not a debate — quizzes are the funnel format that scales best right now. Facebook confirmed it. The reason is segmentation: a quiz asks the customer who they are before you sell.

At V Shred, the quiz ran unchanged for 7 years. No split tests. $300M in revenue. The quiz also unlocks creative diversification — you can test any angle without needing to match your ad to your VSL host.
Segredos da Escala #147 · ~2:47:00
Question from the podcast
"Is the VSL dead? What's the future of direct response funnels?"
VSLs will always exist — the format works. What's changing: the best companies are no longer dependent on one funnel. Diversify or die. V Shred had a quiz funnel, a VSL funnel, and app subscription revenue.

Every additional funnel you make work is an insurance policy on your business. If one gets disrupted — you have others running.
Segredos da Escala #147 · ~2:52:00
Question from the podcast
"What's the most counterintuitive thing you learned about creative testing at scale?"
At V Shred we ran $100K/day and never ran a proper creative split test. What we tested obsessively was the first 3 seconds — because that controls CPC, and CPC controls your ability to scale.

Kevin hired someone in the Philippines to film strange food at the market. Eggs, avocado, bizarre combinations — millions of dollars from that footage.

The counterintuitive lesson: in direct response, weird beats polished every time.
